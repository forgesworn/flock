Legal and safety

flock is a personal, free, non-commercial location-sharing proof of concept for invited adults. It is not a company product or production safety service. These notices set the boundaries that matter before you test it.

Effective 10 July 2026 · Last updated 10 July 2026

Adults only. Do not use flock to track a child.

You must be at least 18 to use flock. Do not install or configure it on a child's device, create an identity for a child, invite a child to a circle, or use it to obtain or share a child's location. This restriction applies even if you are a parent or guardian.

Not an emergency service

flock does not contact police, ambulance, fire, hospitals, rescue services, or public-safety organisations. If someone is in immediate danger, call 999 or 112 in the UK, or use the local emergency number. Keep another way to contact the people you rely on.

Location and delivery can fail

A location can be approximate, stale, delayed, unavailable, or wrong. After you deliberately start sharing in the Android app, it is designed to continue while the phone is locked and in Doze, with a persistent system notification. Battery settings, permissions, GPS conditions, operating-system limits, lost connectivity, relay failure, Tor or VPN failure, a damaged or lost device, and software defects can still interrupt it. Messages and notifications can arrive late or not at all. Do not use flock as your only safety plan.

Consent is not optional

Every adult in a circle must knowingly choose to take part and must control their own device and sharing. Do not hide an installation, pressure someone to join, defeat a device permission, or use flock for stalking, harassment, coercive control, domestic abuse, employee monitoring, or surveillance. A circle invitation is not permission to monitor someone indefinitely.

Why child use is excluded

Location sharing may help families, but it can also expose a child's movements, reduce their private space, or be misused by an unsafe adult. A child-facing version needs a documented best-interests assessment, age-appropriate information and controls, a data protection impact assessment, suitable age and parental-responsibility checks, and different location defaults. The current preview has not completed that work.

A parent who ignores this restriction is responsible for their own breach and misuse. That does not make child use legally invisible: the child is still the person whose location is processed, and law can place separate duties on the service provider that designed and supplied the means. flock therefore uses an adult entry gate and private defaults rather than relying on parental responsibility alone.

Privacy has limits

flock encrypts message and location content for the intended circle and does not require a flock account. That does not make the network invisible. Relays and infrastructure providers may still observe IP addresses, connection times, traffic volume, and destination information. Circle members can see, copy, photograph, or retain anything shared with them. Tor or a VPN changes who can observe an IP address; it does not make a recipient trustworthy.
