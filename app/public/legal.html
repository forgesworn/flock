flock legal and safety notices

flock is preview safety and privacy software. These notices explain the limits that matter before you rely on it.

Last updated: 2026-07-09. This page is a product notice, not legal advice or a complete contract for every jurisdiction.

No emergency services

flock is not an emergency service. It does not contact police, ambulance, fire, hospitals, rescue services, or public-safety organisations. If someone is in immediate danger, use a telephone or another emergency channel such as 112, 999, 911, or the local emergency number.

Location can fail

Location can be approximate, stale, unavailable, delayed, or wrong. Delivery can fail because of battery settings, permissions, GPS availability, operating-system background limits, mobile-network failure, relay outage, Tor/VPN failure, device loss, or bugs. Notifications can arrive late or not at all. Keep a backup safety plan.

Consent and misuse

Do not use flock to track, harass, stalk, coerce, or monitor someone without consent or a lawful guardian basis. Adults should join voluntarily and be able to leave. A parent or guardian using flock with a minor should check local law and explain what is shared in age-appropriate language.

UK/EU prototype limits

flock is a non-commercial proof of concept, not a public child-tracking, workplace-monitoring, medical, elder-care, or emergency-response service. Until there is a formal UK/EU privacy and child-safety review, use it only with adults or parent/guardian-supervised test groups who understand what is shared.

Children may have extra legal protections. In the UK, children under 13 cannot consent to online-service data processing themselves; in the EU, the age can vary by member state. Do not deploy flock as a standing tracker for a child unless you have a lawful basis and have explained the sharing clearly.

Privacy limits

flock minimises what the service can learn. It does not make the network invisible. Encrypted payloads hide message and location content from relays, but network operators can still see metadata such as IP address, timing, bandwidth, destination domain, and connection count unless you route through Tor or a VPN.

This site does not need analytics, advertising pixels, or non-essential cookies. Third parties may include Nostr relays, OpenStreetMap tile/geocoding/Overpass infrastructure, hosting providers, Tor/Orbot, VPNs, Android, browser vendors, remote signers, and notification systems. Their terms and privacy policies may apply.
